Chapter 01 — The Question

What does 20 months of listening actually look like?

Spotify knows a lot about how you listen — but that data stays locked in their app. By requesting the Extended Streaming History export, it's possible to get the raw JSON behind every play, skip, and session. The goal was to go from that messy raw export to a clean, interactive dashboard that tells a real story: not just what was played, but how listening behavior changed over time, when music gets skipped, and what patterns emerge across months and years.

Chapter 02 — The Method

Python for the heavy lifting, Power BI for the story

The pipeline runs in two stages. First, Python handles the raw data — parsing JSON, filtering out podcasts and ads to keep songs only, converting timestamps from UTC to Pacific Time, and engineering temporal features like hour of day, day of week, and 6-month periods. The cleaned CSV then feeds into a 4-page Power BI dashboard with 21 custom DAX measures covering everything from basic KPIs to skip rate trends, artist consistency gauges, and year-over-year comparisons between 2024 and 2025.

Chapter 03 — The Impact

Listening behavior tells a story of growing taste

The most interesting finding: skip rate dropped from 20.55% in 2024 to 12.52% in 2025 — nearly 8 percentage points. That's not random. It points to either better music curation over time, more patient listening habits, or both. The skip patience breakdown reveals that most skips happen within the first 15 seconds, meaning the decision to skip is almost instant — a gut reaction rather than gradual disinterest. Peak listening hours stayed consistent across both years, suggesting stable daily routines that playlists could be built around.
