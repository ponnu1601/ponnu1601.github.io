Chapter 01 — The Question

Who is about to leave, and can we predict it?

Telecom companies lose 15–25% of their customers every year. Most of that churn is preventable — but only if you catch it early. The problem is that retention teams can't call every customer, so they need to know which ones are actually at risk. This project digs into a dataset of 7,043 customers across demographics, services, contracts, and billing patterns to answer one question: what does a customer who is about to leave actually look like?

Chapter 02 — The Method

Exploratory analysis first, then a model that prioritizes recall

The project runs in two stages. First, a thorough exploratory analysis across all 20+ variables — uncovering which customer segments churn most and least. The patterns were striking: month-to-month contract customers churn at 42%, while two-year contract customers churn at just 3%. Fiber optic internet customers churn at 41% versus 19% for DSL. New customers in their first year are especially vulnerable, with churn rates above 50%. These findings shaped the modeling stage, where a Logistic Regression model with class balancing was trained — prioritizing recall to catch as many at-risk customers as possible, even at the cost of some false alarms.

Chapter 03 — The Impact

Four out of five churners caught before they leave

The model flags 4 out of every 5 customers who will actually churn, giving retention teams a precise list to work from rather than guessing. The strongest churn signal is contract type — customers on two-year contracts have a coefficient of -1.554, making it the single most protective factor in the model. The clearest business recommendation: a contract upgrade incentive program targeting month-to-month customers could reduce their churn rate from 42% down to the 3% seen in long-term contracts. With a typical telecom customer lifetime value of $1,500 and a retention campaign cost of $75 per customer, that's a strong return on a targeted outreach effort.
